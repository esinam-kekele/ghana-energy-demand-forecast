# Ghana Energy Demand Forecasting

## Problem Statement

Reliable electricity is critical for economic growth, healthcare, education, and industrial productivity. However, fluctuations in electricity demand can make grid management challenging.

This project develops machine learning and deep learning models to forecast electricity demand using historical energy consumption, weather conditions, and calendar-based features.

The goal is to provide accurate short-term demand forecasts that can support energy planning, operational efficiency, and future smart-grid initiatives in Ghana.

## Objectives

- Forecast electricity demand at different horizons
- Compare statistical, machine learning, and deep learning approaches
- Evaluate model performance under varying seasonal conditions
- Provide interpretable forecasts for decision-makers

## Models

- ARIMA
- XGBoost
- LSTM
- Temporal Fusion Transformer (TFT)

## Impact

Improved forecasting can contribute to:
- Better energy planning
- Reduced operational costs
- Enhanced grid reliability
- Sustainable energy management
